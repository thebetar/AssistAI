To display numbers the 7 segment display was invented because it is the minimum amount of segments needed to represent all the numbers. The udpate version of this was the 14 segment display with also diagonal lines and a plus instead of a line in the middle. Also higher numbers exist

![Untitled](2024-03-22%20948a602f40f44065b27ad4bd1a6a045e/Untitled.png)

The big advantage of higher segment display is that it can not only display numbers but also characters.

A decoder is used to convert binary input into visual output.

A CRT screen takes about 10 microseconds to trace a line and 2 microseconds to retrace

This means that a CRT screen needs 5 nanoseconds per pixel
